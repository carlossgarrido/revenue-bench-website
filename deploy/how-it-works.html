The process

Six steps from role definition through day 90.

The written success profile governs the search. Each stage produces the evidence or alignment needed to begin the next one.

01
Step 01

Define the role and selling environment

We map your buyer, sales motion, deal size, sales cycle, manager expectations, and the way your team wins. The work becomes a written success profile for the person who can perform inside that environment.

That profile keeps sourcing, assessment, interviews, and the final decision pointed at the same standard. It also gives private equity operating partners a consistent basis for hiring across a portfolio.

02
Step 02

Source from the bench and targeted search

We match the role against our pre-vetted bench first. Where the bench does not cover the role, we run a targeted search for the experience, market, or selling motion the profile requires.

The bench means qualified candidates are often in front of you within days, and the slate already fits your selling environment.

03
Step 03

Assess with OMG and an operator screen

Every candidate completes the Objective Management Group sales assessment. It measures will to sell, sales DNA, and whether someone can execute inside your process before you spend time in interviews.

An operator who has built and run sales teams then screens each candidate against the success profile, the selling motion, and the demands of the role.

04
Step 04

Present a true shortlist

You receive a short list of people we would hire ourselves. Every candidate has been assessed and screened against the written success profile.

Each profile comes with assessment findings and our view on fit, giving your interviews a focused decision purpose from the start.

05
Step 05

Place and train

After offer and acceptance, reps complete a two-day sales skills program and leaders complete a leadership refresher through Performance Edge and Sandler.

The hire begins with a shared method, shared language, and clear expectations for how work moves through your sales process.

06
Step 06

Coach for 90 days

A dedicated onboarding coach meets the new hire weekly and the hiring manager weekly for the first 90 days. The cadence follows a structured sales onboarding plan.

The coach keeps expectations, manager feedback, sales method, pipeline work, and deal execution connected through day 90. This addresses the onboarding and management patterns behind many sales hiring failures.

The bench

A standing source of assessed revenue talent.

Revenue Bench sources, assesses, and screens talent continuously. This sales talent bench gives each new search an informed starting point and can shorten the path to a relevant slate.

  • Sellers, leaders, enablement, and RevOps talent sourced continuously.
  • Candidates assessed with OMG and screened by an operator.
  • Matches based on the written selling environment and success profile.
The 90-day replacement guarantee

Ninety days from the hire's start date.

The guarantee runs for the first 90 days from the hire's start date, the same window the onboarding coach is in the work. The full terms are set out in our guide to placement guarantees, and agreed before the search begins.

90-day replacement guarantee
If a hire leaves or is not performing within the first 90 days from the start date, we run the search again at no additional fee. One free re-run, the client's call.
Common questions

Questions about the search process.

How long does it take to hire a salesperson through Revenue Bench?

When the bench covers the role, a true shortlist is commonly ready within 1 to 2 weeks of kickoff. End to end, kickoff to shortlist is commonly 2 to 4 weeks, followed by 90 days of coaching after the hire starts. A pure targeted search can take longer. Timing varies by role, seniority, and how much search is needed beyond the bench.

What happens in the first week?

Revenue Bench and the hiring manager map the buyer, sales motion, deal size, cycle, manager expectations, and success measures. Both sides approve a written success profile, then bench matching and targeted sourcing begin against that standard.

What does the assessment measure?

OMG measures 21 sales-specific competencies across 400+ data points, including will to sell, sales DNA, and tactical skill. Revenue Bench applies it through co-founder Steve Swanston's OMG Certified Partner firm, then adds an operator screen against the written success profile.

How does the 90-day guarantee work?

If a hire leaves or is not performing within the first 90 days, Revenue Bench runs the search again at no additional fee. The terms are agreed before the search begins.

What is included after the hire starts?

Every placement includes a two-day skills program for reps or a leadership refresher for leaders, plus a dedicated onboarding coach who meets the hire and the hiring manager weekly for 90 days.

What if the bench does not have the right candidate?

Revenue Bench runs a targeted search for the experience, market, or selling motion the written profile requires. A pure targeted search usually extends the front half of the timeline beyond the ranges available when the bench covers the role.
